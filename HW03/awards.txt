information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=91st_Academy_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ceremony information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>Due to the last two ceremonies' mixed receptions and declining ratings (with the previous ceremony being the least viewed in the history of the Academy Awards), producers <a href="/wiki/Michael_De_Luca" title="Michael De Luca">Michael De Luca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Todd" title="Jennifer Todd">Jennifer Todd</a> declined to helm the 2019 awards, and were replaced by <a href="/wiki/Donna_Gigliotti" title="Donna Gigliotti">Donna Gigliotti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Weiss" title="Glenn Weiss">Glenn Weiss</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> </p><p>In August 2018, the Academy announced plans to add a new category honoring achievement in "<a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Outstanding_Achievement_in_Popular_Film" title="Academy Award for Outstanding Achievement in Popular Film">Popular Film</a>". The proposal was met with wide criticism, as the award's implied focus on <a href="/wiki/Blockbuster_(entertainment)" title="Blockbuster (entertainment)">blockbuster</a> films was considered to be demeaning towards <a href="/wiki/Art_film" title="Art film">artistic films</a> and other non-mainstream pictures (with the award's title suggesting that such films were not "popular"), that it could diminish the chance for critically successful mainstream films to also be nominated for Best Picture (such as, in particular, <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Panther_(film)" title="Black Panther (film)">Black Panther</a></i>, although the Academy stated that a single film could be nominated in both categories),<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> and for being a ploy to boost ratings.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The Academy announced the following month that it would postpone the new category in order to seek additional input.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Academy president <a href="/wiki/John_Bailey_(cinematographer)" title="John Bailey (cinematographer)">John Bailey</a> admitted that the proposed category was intended to help improve viewership, and noted that the concept of a separate award for commercial film dates back to the <a href="/wiki/1st_Academy_Awards" title="1st Academy Awards">1st Academy Awards</a> (which had separate categories for "Outstanding Picture" and "Best Unique and Artistic Picture").<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> </p><p>In January 2019, it was reported that as part of an effort to shorten the ceremony, only two of the nominees for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Academy Award for Best Original Song">Best Original Song</a> ("<a href="/wiki/All_the_Stars" title="All the Stars">All the Stars</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Shallow_(Lady_Gaga_and_Bradley_Cooper_song)" title="Shallow (Lady Gaga and Bradley Cooper song)">Shallow</a>") would be performed live.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> After a negative reaction from audiences and industry musicians, including <a href="/wiki/Lin-Manuel_Miranda" title="Lin-Manuel Miranda">Lin-Manuel Miranda</a> and members of the music branch,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> the Academy backtracked and announced that all five songs would be performed (although <i>Variety</i> later reported that "All the Stars" would not be performed due to "logistics and timing" issues with the song's performers).<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> </p><p>The following month, the Academy announced that the presentation of the awards for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Cinematography" title="Academy Award for Best Cinematography">Best Cinematography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Live_Action_Short_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Live Action Short Film">Best Live Action Short Film</a>, <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Film_Editing" title="Academy Award for Best Film Editing">Best Film Editing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Makeup_and_Hairstyling" title="Academy Award for Best Makeup and Hairstyling">Best Makeup and Hairstyling</a>—would occur during commercial breaks. They said that these presentations would be streamed so viewers could watch them live online, and that the winners' acceptance speeches would be replayed later in the broadcast. The decision received extensive backlash from audiences, and from filmmakers including <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_del_Toro" title="Guillermo del Toro">Guillermo del Toro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">Christopher Nolan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Damien_Chazelle" title="Damien Chazelle">Damien Chazelle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Dante" title="Joe Dante">Joe Dante</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Cuar%C3%B3n" title="Alfonso Cuarón">Alfonso Cuarón</a> (the latter of whom was nominated and won in one of the aforementioned categories).<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Four days later, the Academy reversed the decision and announced that all 24 categories would be presented live.<sup id="cite_ref-All_Oscar_Categories_to_Air_Live_After_Hollywood_Protest_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_Oscar_Categories_to_Air_Live_After_Hollywood_Protest-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> </p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office_performance_of_Best_Picture_nominees">Box office performance of Best Picture nominees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=91st_Academy_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Box office performance of Best Picture nominees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <table class="wikitable sortable" style="width:350px; The other top 50 box-office hits that earned nominations were <i>Avengers: Infinity War</i> (2nd), <i>Solo: A Star Wars Story</i> (10th), <i>A Quiet Place</i> (15th), <i>Mary Poppins Returns</i> (19th), <i>Ready Player One</i> (24th), and <i>Christopher Robin</i> (34th). </p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Host_selection">Host selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=91st_Academy_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Host selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <p>The <a href="/wiki/90th_Academy_Awards" title="90th Academy Awards">90th Academy Awards</a> in 2018 had the lowest <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_ratings" title="Nielsen ratings">Nielsen ratings</a> of all time, with less than half of the 57.25 million viewers of the <a href="/wiki/70th_Academy_Awards" title="70th Academy Awards">70th in 1998</a>. In October 2018, the Academy asked <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">Dwayne Johnson</a> to host the 91st ceremony, believing that the popularity of Hollywood's <a href="/wiki/List_of_highest_paid_film_actors" title="List of highest paid film actors">highest-paid actor</a> would help increase the audience. Johnson immediately began planning what he described as an "audience first" show, but could not change his schedule of filming <i><a href="/wiki/Hobbs_%26_Shaw" title="Hobbs &amp; Shaw">Hobbs &amp; Shaw</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Untitled_third_Jumanji_film" class="mw-redirect" title="Untitled third Jumanji film">a <i>Jumanji</i> sequel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-keegan20190220_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-keegan20190220-47">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> </p><p>After considering using one host for each of the three hours,<sup id="cite_ref-keegan20190220_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-keegan20190220-47">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> on December 4, 2018, the Academy announced that <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Hart" title="Kevin Hart">Kevin Hart</a> would host the ceremony.<sup id="cite_ref-hart_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hart-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Hart expressed that it was truly an honor and a thrill to be asked to host the Academy Awards.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> A controversy emerged when past jokes and comments made by Hart were found to contain anti-gay slurs and language; Hart withdrew from hosting duties on December 6, saying he did not want to be a "distraction" to the ceremony.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Previous Oscar hosts such as <a href="/wiki/Seth_MacFarlane" title="Seth MacFarlane">Seth MacFarlane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ellen_DeGeneres" title="Ellen DeGeneres">Ellen DeGeneres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neil_Patrick_Harris" title="Neil Patrick Harris">Neil Patrick Harris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rock" title="Chris Rock">Chris Rock</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel" title="Jimmy Kimmel">Jimmy Kimmel</a> expressed no interest in hosting the show.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> </p><p>On January 9, 2019, it was reported that the Academy planned to hold the ceremony without a host, and instead have selected presenters introduce segments and awards. No replacement host was announced, and it became the first ceremony without a designated host since the <a href="/wiki/61st_Academy_Awards" title="61st Academy Awards">61st Academy Awards</a> in 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> </p> <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings_and_reception">Ratings and reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=91st_Academy_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Ratings and reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> <p>Media publications responded more positively to the show than those in recent years. The show also drew a 7.7 rating for the 18–49 demographic.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> </p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Memoriam"><i>In Memoriam</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=91st_Academy_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: In Memoriam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>The annual <i>In Memoriam</i> segment was introduced by the President of the Academy <a href="/wiki/John_Bailey_(cinematographer)" title="John Bailey (cinematographer)">John Bailey</a> with <a href="/wiki/Gustavo_Dudamel" title="Gustavo Dudamel">Gustavo Dudamel</a> conducting the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Philharmonic" title="Los Angeles Philharmonic">Los Angeles Philharmonic</a> on the notes of the "Leaving Home" theme from <i><a href="/wiki/Superman_(1978_film)" title="Superman (1978 film)">Superman</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/John_Williams" title="John Williams">John Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> </p><p>The segment paid tribute to the following 50 artists: </p> <div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em;"> <ul><li><a href="/wiki/Susan_Anspach" title="Susan Anspach">Susan Anspach</a> – actress</li> <li><a href="/wiki/Ermanno_Olmi" title="Ermanno Olmi">Ermanno Olmi</a> – director, writer</li> <li>Richard Greenberg – title designer, visual effects</li> <li><a href="/wiki/John_Carter_(film_editor)" title="John Carter (film editor)">John N.
